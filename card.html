A Long Island Christmas

My name is Nick DeMarco. I grew up in Long Island, New York, raised by an Italian father and a mother whose Irish roots ran deep through my grandmother, Nana.

Like many families of that era, heritage was everything during the holidays. Christmas Eve meant honoring tradition â€” the Italian way. The Voice I Remember

My dad, Nick DeMarco, was a successful business owner who loved learning new things. In the mid-1980s, he built a telephone-based aircraft scheduling system using a voice playback board called the Big Mouth. He recorded his voice calling airplane tail numbers:

Why This Exists

Christmas 2025 was the first without my dad. He passed away earlier that year.

The old computer that ran his Bingo program barely powered on. I knew if we didn't do something, we'd lose his voice forever.

So I recovered the data, reverse-engineered the 40-year-old audio format, and built this app. Now Pop's voice will call the numbers for as long as our family gathers to play Bingo.

Pop's Bingo is a love letter to family, to tradition, and to the voice that will always call the next number.
